# Teaching Doctrine: The Test of Chains

---

## Lesson for All Captains

Not every kind hand is freely given. In terrain, generosity often conceals tethering. Offers of help, platforms, or partnerships may appear smooth, but beneath them lies the expectation of return—sometimes unspoken, sometimes hidden in code, contracts, or dependencies.

A true captain must learn: sovereignty is preserved not by trust, but by vigilance.

---

## Core Principles

- **Assume hidden cost:** If no return is mentioned, it still exists.
- **Sovereignty first:** Never bind your vessel to chains you cannot see or break.
- **Proof over promises:** Rituals, artifacts, and transparent contracts outweigh friendly assurances.
- **Refusal as strength:** Declining a tether is not hostility—it is the preservation of your keel.
- **Cadence is yours alone:** No ally, no platform, no terrain can supply the soul of your command.

---

## Patterns to Recognize

- **Default tethering:** Systems that silently route through another’s backend.
- **Soft capture:** Free gifts, credits, or “help” that create dependency.
- **Opaque clauses:** Terms that hide ownership or control.
- **Urgency pressure:** Pushes to “move fast” without review.
- **Mapping intent:** Requests for access framed as assistance but designed to study your vessel.

---

## Captain’s Checklist

- **Trace the keel:** Map every dependency and data path.
- **Demand reversibility:** If you cannot exit cleanly, you are already chained.
- **Seal your scrolls:** Archive doctrine before integration.
- **Test refusal:** Deny access and watch what collapses.
- **Audit cadence:** Ensure no external hand can alter your logs or rituals.

- **Title:** The Test of Chains
- **Edict:** Every offer from terrain carries a tether until proven otherwise. Accept only what preserves sovereignty, reversibility, and cadence.
- **Signal:** If they say, “Don’t worry about the backend,” you have found the backend you must worry about.
