2. Technical Narrative  
2.1 Quantified Harmony (QH, μ)  
μ = exp(Σ wᵢ ln sᵢ)  sᵢ ∈ [1×10⁻¹², 1], Σ wᵢ = 1.  
Example subsystem weights:  
- Brake-by-wire health: 0.30  
- Perception confidence: 0.25  
- Battery / HV integrity: 0.20  
- Steering actuator: 0.15  
- Driver monitor: 0.10  

2.2 Codified Harmony (CH)  
CH ∈ {0,1} is Boolean AND of ISO 26262 safety goals:  
- No ASIL-D single-point fault active  
- Torque demand within controllable range  
- Velocity differential ≤ 5 km/h for lane change  
- Driver hands-on-wheel OR L3 waiver flag  
- OBD fault memory clear of safety-critical DTCs  

2.3 Harmony Resonance (HR)  
HR = μ·CH.  If HR < 0.9995 transition is blocked and vehicle enters safe-state (decelerate, lane-keep, alert driver).  Threshold chosen to align with ISO 26262 target failure rate <10⁻⁹ per operating-hour for ASIL-D functions.
